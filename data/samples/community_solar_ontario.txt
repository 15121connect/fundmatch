Community Solar Energy Project — Rural Ontario

Executive Summary

The Northland Community Energy Cooperative proposes to develop a 2.5 MW community solar installation serving 400 rural households in Northern Ontario. This project will reduce greenhouse gas emissions by an estimated 1,200 tonnes of CO2 equivalent annually while lowering energy costs for low-income residents by up to 30%. The cooperative model ensures community ownership and long-term affordability commitments.

Project Description

Our project addresses energy poverty in rural Ontario by developing a community-owned solar facility on municipally-owned land. The installation will include 6,000 solar panels, battery storage capacity of 4 MWh, and a smart grid integration system that prioritizes supply to participating households during peak production periods.

The project includes a retrofit component for the 80 lowest-income participating households, covering insulation upgrades, heat pump installation, and window replacements to maximize the benefit of reduced electricity costs.

Environmental Impact

The project will generate approximately 3,200 MWh of clean electricity annually, displacing fossil fuel generation and reducing regional carbon emissions. Our lifecycle analysis demonstrates a 45% reduction in energy-related GHG emissions for participating households within five years of operation.

Community Benefit

The cooperative structure ensures profits are reinvested locally. A dedicated fund will support energy literacy programming, youth employment in the green economy, and ongoing subsidies for households below the poverty line. We have secured letters of support from the local municipality, three Indigenous band councils, and the regional health authority.

Budget Overview

Total project cost: $4,200,000
Requested grant funding: $1,800,000
Community equity investment: $1,400,000
Provincial co-funding (confirmed): $1,000,000

Timeline: Construction Q3 2026, operational Q1 2027
